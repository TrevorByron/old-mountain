01 — Identity

The Logo.

Primary usage

Primary — on dark backgrounds
Secondary — on olive/green backgrounds
Dark ground — deep field

Clear space & minimums

Always maintain clear space equal to the height of the circular emblem on all sides.

  • Min digital Full lockup: 120px wide minimum. Icon circle alone: 32px minimum.
  • Min print 1 inch / 25mm wide minimum for the full horizontal lockup.
  • File formats Use .svg for all digital. Export .png @2x for social platforms.

Logo don'ts

Don't recolor the logo

Don't stretch or distort

Don't place on cream/light backgrounds

Don't apply opacity or tint

Color roles

  • Dark / Deep — 80% of all backgrounds
  • Cream — primary text, occasional panel backgrounds
  • Gold — accent only. Use sparingly. Never fill large areas.
  • Olive — secondary accent. CTA panels, green variation.

Accessibility

  • → Cream on Dark passes WCAG AA at all sizes (contrast 13.2:1)
  • → Gold on Dark passes AA at large text only — use for headlines, kickers
  • → Text Dark on Cream passes AAA (contrast 17.6:1)
  • → Never place small body text in gold — use cream

From the ancient traditions of the Hadzabe to the vast Serengeti plains — this journey is designed around what Old Mountain believes is the very best Tanzania has to offer. Built by people who call Africa home.

Rules

  • awan-zaman is the primary display face — use for all hero headlines and section titles
  • Sunday April is exclusively used as the italic emphasis within awan-zaman lines (the <em> tag)
  • Chainprinter is always uppercase, always letter-spaced, used for labels, kickers, and UI chrome only — never for body text
  • System UI / Roboto handles all body copy — never use awan-zaman or Chainprinter for paragraph text
  • Headline hierarchy: 64–96px display · 40–56px section title · 24–32px sub-head

Don'ts

  • Don't use Sunday April alone — only as em within awan-zaman
  • Don't use Chainprinter at sizes above 13px or without letter-spacing
  • Don't set awan-zaman in lowercase for headlines — mixed case for phrases, sentence case is fine
  • Don't use bold or italic on awan-zaman — it has no weight variants
  • Don't use more than 3 typefaces in any single composition

04 — Imagery

The Photography.

Do

  • Authentic, candid moments — people genuinely in the experience
  • Landscape-scale photography that conveys vastness and awe
  • Golden hour and blue hour light — warm tones, long shadows
  • Wildlife in natural context — not close-up zoo-style shots
  • Environmental portraits — guides, hosts, community members
  • Dark, moody processing — slightly underexposed, rich shadows
  • Real photos from actual trips — no stock photography

Don't

  • Stock photography or generic "safari" imagery
  • Bright, overexposed, heavily HDR processed images
  • Tourists posing in safari gear for the camera
  • Wildlife-only shots that feel like National Geographic stock
  • Images dominated by logos, watermarks, or branding
  • Overly saturated or filtered images (Instagram filter aesthetic)
  • Photos that could have been taken by anyone, anywhere in Africa

Color treatment

Images should be treated consistently: slight desaturation, crushed blacks, warm highlights. The goal is "filmic" not "vivid." Think old Kodak slide film, not iPhone default processing.

Aspect ratios

  • 3:2 — landscapes, wide scenes
  • 4:5 — Instagram portrait posts
  • 1:1 — Instagram square, profile
  • 9:16 — Stories, Reels
  • 16:9 — YouTube thumbnails

Overlay treatment

When text is placed on photography, always use a dark gradient overlay (bottom to transparent or full field dimming). Never place text directly on an unmodified photo.

05 — Tone

The Voice.

Personal

We write the way brothers talk. Direct, warm, specific. This isn't a company speaking — it's Jesse and Trevor, people who grew up in East Africa and are genuinely excited to share it.

Grounded

We earned this. Every route, every lodge, every Hadzabe connection comes from decades of real experience. We let that speak without boasting about it.

Precise

We use specifics over superlatives. Not "incredible views" — "the world's largest intact volcanic caldera." Not "amazing local culture" — "time with the Hadzabe, one of the last hunter-gatherer peoples on earth."

Unhurried

We don't push. We invite. There's no urgency copy, no countdown timers, no "limited time." People who are right for these trips will feel it — we just need to be honest and clear.

Not corporate

We are not a travel agency. No "world-class experiences," no "turnkey solutions," no "seamless journey." Old Mountain is two brothers who know Africa — write like it.

Not Instagram-travel

Avoid performative adventure language: "epic," "bucket list," "jaw-dropping," "life-changing." These trips are all of those things — but the photos and specifics should do that work, not the adjectives.

Copy examples

Do say

"Built by people who call Africa home, it's an epic, deeply considered experience you can feel confident in."

Don't say

"An unforgettable world-class safari experience crafted by industry experts with decades of expertise."

Do say

"A rare and extraordinary day living alongside and learning from the Hadzabe community."

Don't say

"An amazing once-in-a-lifetime cultural immersion opportunity you simply can't miss!"

How to export

Browser screenshot

Open any template file in Chrome, open DevTools, click "Toggle device toolbar," set custom dimensions matching the asset size, then screenshot with ⌘+Shift+P → "Capture full size screenshot."

Figma/Canva

Use the brand guide colors and fonts directly in Figma or Canva. Import awan-zaman and Chainprinter via the fonts folder, or substitute Canva's "Archivo" + a script font with the correct visual weight.

Puppeteer / Playwright

For automated batch exports, use Puppeteer or Playwright to open each template at its defined viewport size and screenshot to PNG at 1x (dimensions already match the required pixel output).
